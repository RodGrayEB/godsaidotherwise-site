The Journey

Three Chapters of an Uncommon Life

Chapter I · 2020–2024

The Weight of the Storm

Kidney failure arrives without warning. Roderick begins dialysis — five years of it — while continuing to teach, to father, to believe. Then 2024 brings something no one was ready for.

Chapter II · 2024–2025

Into the Valley

Five months hospitalized. His ability to walk: gone. His faith: tested beyond measure. His family: immovable. What happens in the darkest places determines who you become in the light.

Chapter III · November 2025

God Said Otherwise

Step by step through intensive rehabilitation at Shirley Ryan. And then — the call every transplant patient prays for. A second chance. A kidney. A life reclaimed. God had answered.

"The Doctors Gave Me Odds.
God Gave Me Otherwise."

Roderick Gray's memoir is a testimony to what faith, family, and friendship can carry you through — even when medicine says the chances are slim.

About the Author

Roderick Gray

Educator · Survivor · Author · Speaker

Born and raised on Chicago's South Side, Roderick Gray taught for 30 years in the West Englewood community — the same community that forged him. He holds a BA in Economics and an MA in Sociology from Western Illinois University, and an MA in Education from Roosevelt University.

He is the devoted husband of Tene and the proud father of Roderick Jr., William, and Amir. His survival story is a gift to everyone who has ever been told the odds are against them.

"I didn't survive so I could stay quiet. I survived so I could tell the truth about what carried me."
